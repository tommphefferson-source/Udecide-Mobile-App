Application Overview

UDecide is a mobile app that helps U.S. voters understand their government and participate in elections. It brings together a citizen's elected representatives, upcoming elections, active legislation, voter registration tools, and civic education — all presented from a strictly nonpartisan standpoint. The app never ranks parties, endorses candidates, or editorializes.

How it works

Everything in the app is driven by the user's location. After creating an account, the user provides their address; the app then tailors representatives, elections, and legislation to their federal, state, county, and city jurisdictions. A location override lets users explore any other U.S. address without changing their profile.

Technology at a glance

Section 1

Account & Sign-in

Sign In

  • Email + password sign-in against the UDecide member system.
  • Password visibility toggle and inline validation with clear error messages.
  • Sign in with Google — one-tap sign-in. Google verifies the identity on the server; the app can never forge an account. On the web build this opens a secure popup; on phones it uses the system browser.
  • New Google users are guided through a short "Almost there" step (city + ZIP) to finish creating their account.
  • Sessions persist securely on the device; users stay signed in between launches.

Create Account

  • Collects name, email, password, and location (city, state, ZIP).
  • Validation on every field before submission.
  • After registration, a profile-setup step captures the full street address that powers representative and election matching.

Forgot Password

  • Self-service reset: the user enters their account email and receives reset instructions.

Registration flow

Account creation runs through the secure API server to the UDecide member system. Duplicate emails and invalid entries return friendly, actionable messages. All traffic between the app and the API server is encrypted (HTTPS).

Section 2

Dashboard (Home)

The dashboard is the user's civic home base — a card-driven layout that surfaces the most relevant information and provides one-tap access to every feature.

  • Personalized greeting and the user's current civic location.
  • Quick-access cards to Representatives, Elections, Legislation, Voter Tools, Polls, Fact Checker, and more.
  • Political news access via the member news feed, opened as readable in-app articles.
  • Reference links (state/town resources) supplied by the member system.
  • Nonpartisanship notice reinforcing the app's neutral stance.
All personalized content follows the user's effective location — their profile address, or the temporary override set in Location Override (Section 12).

Section 3

Representatives

See every elected official who represents you — from the President down to city council — based on your exact address.

  • Officials grouped by level: Federal, State, County, City with an easy filter row.
  • Live data from the Cicero civic-data service, requested securely through the API server.
  • Each official shows their photo, office, party affiliation, and contact channels (phone, email, website, social).
  • Detail view includes term information and, where available, voting-record context.
  • Updates automatically when the user's effective location changes.

Section 4

Elections & Ballots

A forward-looking view of every election that affects the user, with the practical details needed to participate.

  • Upcoming federal, state, and local elections for the user's jurisdictions.
  • Key dates: election day, registration deadlines, early-voting windows, and absentee deadlines.
  • Candidate listings presented neutrally — no endorsements or rankings.
  • Countdown context so users know how soon each election is.

Section 5

Legislation Tracker

Follow bills as they move through the user's state legislature and Congress.

  • State legislation from the LegiScan service; federal data from Congress.gov.
  • Bill cards show number, title, plain-language status (Introduced, In Committee, Passed, Signed, etc.), and last action.
  • Search and filtering to find bills by topic.
  • Detail view summarizes the bill and its progress timeline.

More Menu

The fifth tab collects the app's remaining tools and account controls in one organized menu.

Voter Tools

Practical, state-aware guidance that turns intent into a cast ballot.

  • Voter-registration status guidance and links to official state registration resources.
  • Polling-place information for the user's address.
  • Absentee / mail-in voting rules and deadlines for the user's state.
  • Early-voting availability and windows.

Section 10

Polls & Issue Questionnaires

Community Polls

Community Polls

  • Members vote on current civic questions from the UDecide community.
  • One vote per member per poll; results are viewable after voting.
  • Live results with vote counts and percentages.

Issue Questionnaires

  • Structured issue sets that let users record where they stand on policy topics.
  • Stances are private to the user — an informational self-assessment, not a public poll.
Issue Questionnaire
Section 11

AI Fact Checker

A conversational assistant, powered by Google's Gemini AI, purpose-built for neutral political fact-checking.

  • Users paste a claim or ask a question; the assistant responds with factual, sourced-style context.
  • A strict nonpartisan system prompt keeps answers neutral — the assistant describes, it never advocates.
  • Streaming, chat-style interface with conversation history during the session.
  • Clear disclaimer that AI answers are informational and users should verify with primary sources.

Section 12

Profile & Location Override

Profile

Profile

  • Edit name, contact details, and the home address that drives all location-based features.
  • Profile photo upload from the device photo library.
  • Changes save to the UDecide member system and take effect across the app immediately.

Location Override

  • Explore the political landscape of any U.S. address — representatives, elections, and legislation for that location — without changing the saved profile.
  • A visible indicator shows when an override is active; one tap returns to the home address.
